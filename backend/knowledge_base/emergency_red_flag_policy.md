# Emergency Red Flag Policy

This document supports safe urgency routing only. It does not diagnose disease, prescribe medicine, or replace emergency services.

Emergency red flags should trigger immediate emergency-care guidance before routine appointment booking. Patients should be told to contact local emergency services or visit the nearest emergency department.

Emergency red flags include chest pain with breathing difficulty, difficulty breathing, severe bleeding, loss of consciousness, confusion, stroke-like symptoms, one-sided weakness, facial drooping, severe allergic reaction, swelling of the face or throat, pregnancy bleeding, severe pregnancy abdominal pain, suicidal thoughts, seizure, severe head injury, severe burns, blue lips, severe dehydration in a child, and high fever in an infant.

The routing system must never reassure an emergency case as routine. It should clearly state that the tool does not provide diagnosis and that urgent medical assessment may be needed.
