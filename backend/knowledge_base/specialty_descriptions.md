# Specialty Descriptions

This document supports safe routing only. It does not diagnose disease, prescribe medicine, or replace a qualified doctor.

## General Physician

A General Physician may be suitable for common symptoms, routine health concerns, fever, cough, body aches, mild stomach symptoms, general weakness, and first-contact medical review. When symptoms are unclear or involve several body systems, a General Physician can help decide the next appropriate referral.

## Cardiologist

A Cardiologist may be suitable for heart and circulation concerns such as chest discomfort, palpitations, fainting episodes, or blood pressure follow-up. Chest pain with breathing difficulty is treated as an emergency routing concern and should trigger immediate emergency-care guidance.

## Dermatologist

A Dermatologist may be suitable for skin, hair, and nail concerns such as rash, itching, acne, skin irritation, and chronic skin changes.

## ENT Specialist

An ENT Specialist may be suitable for ear, nose, throat, sinus, voice, and upper respiratory discomfort, including sore throat, ear pain, blocked nose, sinus pressure, or persistent throat symptoms.

## Dentist

A Dentist may be suitable for tooth pain, gum pain, oral discomfort, dental checkups, and non-emergency dental concerns.

## Pediatrician

A Pediatrician may be suitable for infants, children, and adolescents. High fever in infants, severe dehydration in children, breathing difficulty, seizure, or altered consciousness should trigger emergency routing.

## Gynecologist

A Gynecologist may be suitable for pregnancy-related symptoms, menstrual concerns, pelvic discomfort, and reproductive health routing. Pregnancy bleeding or severe pregnancy abdominal pain should trigger emergency routing.

## Orthopedic

An Orthopedic doctor may be suitable for joint pain, back pain, bone injury, sports injury, suspected fracture, and musculoskeletal concerns.

## Psychiatrist

A Psychiatrist may be suitable for anxiety, panic symptoms, sleep problems, mood concerns, and mental-health support. Suicidal thoughts should trigger emergency guidance and immediate support.

## Ophthalmologist

An Ophthalmologist may be suitable for eye pain, sudden blurry vision, red eye, or other eye symptoms. Sudden vision changes or severe eye pain may require urgent routing.

## Gastroenterologist

A Gastroenterologist may be suitable for stomach pain, vomiting, diarrhea, digestion concerns, and liver or digestive-system routing.

## Urologist

A Urologist may be suitable for urinary pain, kidney-area pain, urinary symptoms, and blood in urine routing.
